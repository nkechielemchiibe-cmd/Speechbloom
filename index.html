🔒 Keeping Your Child Safe on YouTube

SpeechBloom opens videos in your browser — not the YouTube app. This means you can use a browser extension to hide the search bar completely before handing the device to your child.

⭐ Recommended — Unhook Extension (Android Chrome and Desktop)

Unhook is a free browser extension that hides the YouTube search bar, recommendations and comments. Once installed, your child cannot search for anything on YouTube.

Step 1 — Open Chrome on your device and go to the Chrome Web Store. Search for Unhook and install it.

Step 2 — Open Unhook settings and toggle Hide Search Bar to On.

Step 3 — Done. Every video your child taps in SpeechBloom will open in Chrome with no search bar visible.

Method 2 — Enable Restricted Mode

Filters out most mature content from search results on all devices.

On Android: Tap Profile Picture → Settings → General → turn Restricted Mode On.

On desktop: Click Profile Picture → Restricted Mode → Activate. You can lock it so your child cannot turn it off.

Method 3 — Set Up a Supervised Account via Google Family Link

For children under 13. Lets you choose exactly what content the search bar returns.

Open Google Family Link → Select your child → Controls → YouTube → Content settings → Choose Explore for the most restricted experience.

Method 4 — Pause and Clear Search History

Prevents the search bar from showing trending videos or past searches when your child taps it.

In Family Link → General Settings for your child → Toggle Pause search history to On → tap Clear history.

Method 5 — Use YouTube

The only platform that lets you turn off Search completely. Install YouTube from the Play Store → tap the Lock icon → select your child's profile → turn Search off. Your child can only watch what appears on screen.
